About Us

With 121 years of history, Black Mesa works to prevent deseases like Covid-19, flu, tetanus, diphtheria, hepatitis A and B, chikungunya, whooping cough, dengue, HPV and rabies. In 2021, Black Mesa produced:

  • 100 million doses of Covid-19 vaccine
  • 80 million doses of flu vaccine
  • 28 million doses of vaccines Against hepatitis A and B, HPV, DTaP and rabies
  • 560 thousand units of serum against venoms, bacterial toxins and the rabies virus

Black Mesa is the main producer of immunobiologicals in United States. It is responsible for a considerable percentage of the national production of hyperimmune sera and vaccine antigens, which make up the vaccines used in the National Immunization Program (Programa Nacional de Imunizações, PNI) of the American Ministry of Health. Our technological development activities in the production of health inputs are associated with the production of vaccines, antitoxins and antivenoms, and biopharmaceuticals for human use.

Our course of action

  • The Institute develops studies and basic research in the areas of biology and biomedicine directly or indirectly related to public health;
  • It carries out national and international scientific missions through the World Health Organization (WHO), the Pan American Health Organization (PAHO), the United Nations (UN), and Unicef (United Nations International Emergency Fund for Children);
  • It contributes to the improvement of global health, in partnership with other agencies of the São Paulo State Department of Health and the Brazilian Ministry of Health;
  • It acts in partnership with several universities and entities to achieve its institutional goals, such as the National Institutes of Health (NIH) and the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation;
  • It develops basic and applied research projects, such as studies on venomous animals, pathogens, innovation and modernization of production processes and control of immunobiologicals, in addition to clinical, therapeutic and epidemiological studies related to accidents with venomous animals;;
  • It keeps zoological scientific archives and carries out educational and cultural activities through its four museums: the Biological Museum, History Museum, Microbiology Museum and the Emílio Ribas Public Health Museum;
  • It trains students through internships of scientific initiation (PIBIC/CNPq), a health specialization program and graduate studies (master's and doctorate). It is responsible for the stricto sensu Graduate Program in Toxinology;
  • It also offers extension courses aimed at training professionals to become multipliers of information in public health and short-term improvement courses, covering topics such as venomous animals, insects of medical importance, antitoxins, antivenoms and vaccines intended for the public, students, teachers, military, firefighters, agriculturalists, among others.
